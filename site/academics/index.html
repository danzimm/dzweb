Education

Research

Asymptotic Limits of Nonlocal Curvature for Curves

Loyola University Chicago · B. Seguin · 2023–present

With B. Seguin, I study a nonlocal curvature functional for curves in Rn. The central question is whether, as the interaction scale tends to zero, the nonlocal quantity converges to classical curvature.

For circles, I have verified this recovery explicitly. I am now working on the corresponding convergence statement for sufficiently regular curves. A manuscript is in progress.

Supernova Photometric Contamination Studies

Argonne National Laboratory · S. Kuhlmann, J. Cunningham · 2013–2015

At Argonne National Laboratory, I studied contamination in photometrically selected Type Ia supernova samples. I used SNANA simulations, SALT2 parameter-space cuts, and SNCosmo template fitting to separate Type Ia candidates from core-collapse supernovae with similar light curves.

By refining the light-curve classification procedure, I reduced false-positive Type Ia identifications by approximately 50%. The 2015 AAS session poster and supporting analysis code are available online.

Current interests

I am interested in geometric analysis, nonlocal-to-local limits, variational definitions of geometric quantities, and the way physical models suggest useful mathematical structure.

I also use computation as a preliminary instrument: not as a substitute for proof, but as a way to test definitions, identify examples, and determine which conjectures are coherent enough to formalize.
